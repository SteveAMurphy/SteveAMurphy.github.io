Audio / Video

As part of my Masters in Interaction and Experience Design, I have had the opportunity to work on a number of auido and video based projects. And they have been hugely enjoyable.

I have thoroughly enjoyed using the different software packages introduced to us throughout the course so far. I have used a number of these packages before while working on personal projects but I feel I am now armed with just enough exposure to the "best practise" in these software packages to be able to create genuinely useful content.

I am happy for the most part in the content I have produced but I am honest enough to admit that far more practice will be needed to take this content to the level acceptable in industry.

My Sound Story

The following clip was created using only audio clips available online. A total of 23 clips were merged, edited and joined to form an audio story.

The Narrative

It was early spring, the man made his way through the wood to the clearing. Dropping his bag, he set about the work of digging the hole. When the hole was deep enough, he set the young tree in place, watered it liberally, collected his belongings and left the clearing to give the space back to nature. Time passed by, as it always does.

The man returns to the clearing. Once again he sets about the task at hand. The growl of the chainsaw rips through the crisp autumn air. The tree falls. With a freshly sharpened axe, the man splits the wood. When done, he makes his way to the shelter of the cabin. Inside, the man clears last nights fire and lights the kindling. The fire takes hold of the logs, crackling as the fresh sap is ignited and the timber cracks with the steam. He moves to the kitchen and pours a glass of wine before retiring on the couch. His days work done.

The Goal

The goal of the project was to make a sound story using only original or free audio samples. The aim was to create a story that the listener could understand without any visual propmt or description.

Challenges

Something I struggled with was the passing of time, I tried using seasonal nature sounds but because the background audio was also in nature, it didn't work. Another dfficulty was the fact I knew what I was trying to express, so I had to keep getting new ears to listen because I had both a bias and prior knowledge and could not be critical enough.

Product Demo

This was a group project with two other class members. As part of a separate module, the group designed a document management system. The system was based on a digital application to capture, manage and store both physical and digital documents. The video below is a Product Demo of the prototype.

Software used

The prototype was created in Figma and the video was created and edited using Adobe Premier Pro, Canva and Adobe After Effects with graphics generated using Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator

Reflection

I keep referring to a point made by a guest lecture during a presentation on video content creation. He said "Even your grandmother knows what good video is supposed to look like in this day and age". He is right. The quality of output expected is at the highest level because of the amount of good examples out there. Therefore I am committed to improving with every piece of content I make. I plan to engage more with creative tools like Premier-Pro and After-Effects as I develop.
