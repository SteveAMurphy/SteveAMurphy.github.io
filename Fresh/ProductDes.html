Navu

Navu is a re-deisgn of a firefighters SCBA mask to include crew accountability and navigation features as well as system diagnostics.

This design uses movement sensors which are embedded in the firefighters clothing to track the movement of the firefighter as they navigate the inside of a building. The location tracking is processed and the information sent to a uint in teh firefighters SCBA mask. This unit then projects the information on the inside of the firefighters mask so they have an augmented vision of their surroundings.

The system can also be used to inform the firefighrter of their remaining air supply, give the firefighter thermal imagiing overlayt of their surroundings and it will give them a reference map for when they need to re-trace their steps back out of the building

Feedback from firefightes has been very positive and although the system was designed as an exploration inot how current state of the art technology could be used to help this particular group of users, it is only a speculative design exercise.

The render was developed in a number of different orientations to best communicate the geometry of the design. This helped when creating the CAD model.

Image Manipulation

Using Photoshop and Illustrator. The image above has been manipulated to give the impression that the projector is operational in an effort to explain to the viewer the function of the product.

I thoroughly enjoyed the process of vreating the Vector file of the NAVU logo and then using Photoshop to make it appear to be projected onto the foreground.

Photoshoot

When the representation model was completed, I took it to one of my research participants for a photoshoot and to get opinion on the final design.

The feedback was veruy encouraging. It was great to see the reaction to the final design solution and this helped convince the user to put on the mask and take some shots for the process book

Mask Casting

In order to get the correct dimensions for the mask, I made a cast of my face. I could have used a mannequin head, but they are nor correctly proportioned, so I used mine. A resin mold was vreated and a plaster paris cast of my face was used to build a clay model of the mask.

This was a time consuming process and a mannequin head might have been a compromise worth making but it was worth doing just to get expereince of casting.

Final Model Build

The final model was a blend of 3d printed parts, cnc machined foam parts, hand shaped foam parts, vacuum formed plastic and laser cut components. These were all prepped and painted and tehn assembled to create the representatinoal model used for the photshoot and for demonstration purposes.

I'm really happy with the outcome of this endeavour. I don't think I would change much about the process. It was a great learning experience.

Bijur Hydraulic Pump

This project was undertaken in a collaboration with Bijur Delimon Pumps

The client, Bijur Delimon were launching a new pump and needed help with the Industrial Design of the pump. The mechaincs of the pump had already been configured so the scope of the design was on the aesthetic features and the pump conmtrol interface.

Our team used participatory design to help the client develop the design and appreance they wanted for the pump.

The outcome was a modern, industrially designed pump which was a radical move from the clients traditional pump design but which helped the new pump stand out from the competion. The product has been on the market for a number of years now and the desing is still a point of very positive discussion.

On reflection, I would love to have another go at the user interface of the pump when I am finished my current postgraduate degree to see what improvements can be made. The labelling of the buttons is a weakness in my view.
