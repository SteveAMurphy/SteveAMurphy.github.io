Still Life

Somewhere on the Wild Atlantic Way. The views were unbelievable

This image represents the fun things in life. Toys, imagination, recreation, and indeed life goals are all represented in the image of this motorcycle cop as he diligently goes about his daily duties, protecting the citizens of Big Block City.

Composition:

The image follows the rule of thirds. The motorcyclists eye is at the intersection of the upper third and the right third. The items in the background represent the sprawling metropolis and the depth of field makes the main subject stand out. The items and the main subject are arranged so that the source of light for the image corresponds with the painted light reflection on the motorcycle and motorcyclist. Different height blocks at different distances from the camera were used to add depth to the image.

The aperture settings were restricted on my camera set-up, I would have preferred a bit more depth of field. If I was trying to improve the image, I would proably add a little more fore-ground so that the shadow of the cyclist is not cut so severely on the right.

Camera Details:
  • Sony 6500
  • 16-50mm lens
  • ISO - 160
  • Shutter Speed 1/60
  • Aperture f3.5
Life in Metaphor

“Everyone goes with the flow, but the one who goes against the flow becomes someone remarkable in life” Swami Vivekananda

The flow of water represents the flow of life, in this case, it is a torrent, a whirling unstoppable force of life. At times we try to swim against the flow, the branch represents the futile nature of trying to fight the passage of time.

I wanted to get an image that showed movement. Photographs record a single moment in time, I saw this as a challenge to try and give the image the apprearance of movement.

Composition:

The image is roughly split into the traditional rule of thirds, with the tree-line in the background and the suggested line from the stone wall in the foreground splitting the image into horizontal thirds. The bushes either side and the alignment of the branch in the water with the edge of the stone also split the image.The image needed a foreground to anchor the image so the stone wall remained in the lower left corner. The trees at the far side of the river form the horizon line and were straightened in post processing.Finally, rather than have the flow of water from left to right which is preferred for viewing, the water flows from right to left to further enhance the battle of the branch against the water flow.

Camera Details:
  • Sony 6500
  • 16-50mm lens
  • ISO - 160
  • Shutter Speed 1/2
  • Aperture f22
Life Source

“It is life I think to watch the water, a man can learn so many things” Nicholas Sparks

All life on earth came from water. It truly is the source of all life. As if to prove this, the image of the stream shows the abundance of life that grows and exists because of water. Like the Nile delta in Egypt, this little stream provides nutrients and water for the wealth of greenery it flows through.

Composition

The sound of the small waterfall drew my attention so I went off the pathway and found this lovely pool of Life. I took a number of test pictures and adjusted my settings with the intention of shooting as much of the scene as possible and then cropping later in post. Using the rule of thirds as a guide, I made the stream the center of the images, the water then flows slightly to the left before coming back in the calm water flowing from left to right. The leaves frame the upper third and the two tree trunks provide some symmetry. The picture was taken from an angle that allowed some of the suns rays to hit the pool of water and bounce a reflection to provide even more life to the image

I ended up cropping this image quiet a bit more that I would have liked. I think it lost some of the detail because of this. If re-taking it, I would frame it slightly better and not have to cut so much from the borders.

  • ISO - 800
  • Shutter Speed 1/50
  • Aperture f3.5
Life in a Nut-shell

“Enormous Oak trees from tiny acorns grow”

This image represents the start of life, as the Autumn season progresses this tiny seed will fall from the Oak tree and if nurtured by nature, will grow to become a mighty Oak tree, thus the circle of life continues. Life exists in all living things and nature is full of living things. Over millions of years the Oak tree has evolved its seed to the size and shape we see in the image. Out of the thousands or millions of tree species, the acorns form is relatively unique.

Composition

The simplicity of the acorn draws the eye. It stands out from the leafy background. The main focal point is in the top right section of the image, conforming to the rule of thirds. The colour of the acorn sets it apart from the surrounding green of the leaves, again drawing the eye. The small water droplets on the acorn also seem to pull the viewer further into the images. Shutter speed was kept low so that any blurring of the moving branch in the breeze would be reduced. The image reveals itself in three stages: we see the acorn first, then the water droplet as we focus more on the acorn and then the rest of the image . The subject of the images is close to the camera to cut out too many distractions, then further cropped in post.

There is something that bugs me about this image. The texture of the nut and the water droplet on it just seems to be out of focus. When I zoom in, I dont think it is, but at this focal length, it jsut seems off. I'd love to go back with a macro lens and shoot it again. Although that nut is long gone!!!

Life in a Nut-shell

Light give life to a dark night, it gives life to a gloomy pathway. Light makes shadows dance and light itself dances on the surface of the browning autumn leaves. The lamp in the image gives life to the foliage which surrounds and encircles the lamp, almost enveloping it as if to try and share in the warmth of the lamps glow.

Although the leaves may be fading as the winter draws near, there is still the appearance of life in the green leaves in the background. A hope for life yet to be lived.

Composition

This image attempted to find the natural sequence (Fibonacci’s sequence in nature). The light falling on the leaves follows the sequence. After experimenting with trying to get the reflection of the lamp and leaves in a puddle of water, I decided to take the shot directly at the lamp and change the camera setting to counter the strong light coming from the lamp.

Although I love the colours in teh image and I am happy to have gotent a decent crack at the Fibonacci composition, the image looks a little thron together. At the time of shootinmg there was a puddle of water developing under the lamp post. I would have loved to have gotten a refelction shot on the puddle with the colours of the leaves. Maybe next year.
