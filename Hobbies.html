The base vehicle

2011 VW Transporter. Foremely a Guiness van.

The VW van ys synonimous with campers. The 1960's split screens were the first true Hippy Vans. This was a natural selection as the base vehicle for the camper project.

Windows In

Probably the scariest part of any van build. Cutting huge holes in the side. But the rreward is worth the risk. Goodbye sardine can, hello windows to the world.

Pop-top

What a difference, the opo-top adds the benefit of interiro height. PFitting a pop-top to the van means doubling the sleeping capacity, but it also means cooking and living in the van is so much more comfortable.

Cooking Facilities

The van is now equipped with cooking and cleaning facilities. This involved wiring, plumbing and gas piping. I had it all checdked out by preofessionals and was haqppy that it was all approved.

Finished Van with awning

On a maiden voyage to west Clare. Drive-away anwing adds the freedom of taking short day trips. Well worth the time to pitch, especially with the easy to use inflatable beams.

Finished Interior

Equippedn with all mode-cons. Nespresso machine, heating, sink, hob and 12v with 240v backup. It all came together nicely in the end
